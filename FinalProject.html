Greek Mythology

Zeus: King of the Gods

As the king of the gods Zeus is considered the most powerful of all the Olympian gods. When he and his brothers: Poseidon and Hades drew lots to see who would be ruler of each domain, Zeus drew the sky. Zeus is the ruler of the sky,
able to control thunder and what he is most well known for; lightning.
Zeus came into power when he defeated his father; Kronos, Titan of time. His father did not want to lose power and so every child himself and his wife Rhea had, he ate.
Since the gods are immortal, these children were alive inside Kronos' stomach until Zeus, their youngest brother who had been substituted as a rock by his mother when he was born to escape being eaten by his father,
fed Kronos a mixture of mustard and wine.
This concoction made Kronos puke up his siblings, and a fight ensued, ending with Zeus and his siblings' victory over their father.

Hera: Queen of the Gods

Although the sister of Zeus, godly relationships do not work in the same manner as humans, and so Zeus and Hera married, just as their parents; Kronos and Rhea were siblings before them.
Hera is known as the Goddess of marriage which is quite unfortunate considering her husband is the most well-known entity in Greek Mythology for cheating.
Together, Hera and Zeus had many children, including the Olympian gods Ares, God of War, and Hepheastus, God of the Forge.
Hera is well known for her jealousy and revenge against the many lovers of Zeus, often cursing the women with punishments, even if Zeus' advances were not welcome.

Ares: God of War

Ares is the son of the King and Queen of Olympus, Zeus and Hera. He is the god of War and is well known for his brutality and bloodlust.
He is a master of all weapons, and one of the most formidable of the gods. Ares has been known to start conflicts throughout time just for the sake of bloodshed.
It is believed he enjoys starting conflict between family memebrs the most, as there is nothing messier than blood spilt between family.
Ares has a relationship with Aphrodite, Goddess of Love, despite her marriage to his brother, Hepheastus, God of the Forge.
